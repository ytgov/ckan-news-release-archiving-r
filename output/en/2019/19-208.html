Yukon Corporate Online Registry delivers next phase of e-service

Published October 1, 2019

The Government of Yukon is reducing red tape and moving to a faster, electronic environment for Yukon businesses with the launch of the next phase of the Yukon Corporate Online Registry (YCOR).

In addition to the current online services on YCOR, such as being able to apply to reserve business names, clients can now also submit periodic reports such as annual returns, changes of directors and registered addresses regarding local and extra-territorial business corporations.

Our next step is online functions such as annual returns and notices of change of directors and registered address for societies. These will be available later this year.

Yukon Corporate Online Registry provides businesses and societies with modern tools to submit ongoing filings for transparency to better serve the public interest. By reducing paperwork, we are reducing red tape in this Yukon. This new e-service will increase access to services and free up time for the many businesses and societies that make important contributions to the quality of life for all Yukoners. 

Minister of Community Services John Streicker
Quick facts

  • In March 2017, the initial phase of Yukon Corporate Online Registry was launched to provide free searches of business entities’ status, followed by paid searches for business entities’ profiles and filings, and the ability to print up-to-date certificates of status and compliance for a fee.

  • Advanced searches and online renewal of business names were later added.

  • Latest online features include applications for name reservations, filing of annual reports, notices of change of directors and registered addresses for local business corporations.

  • Corporate Affairs’ original corporate registry desktop system has been migrated to a new web-based system that allows real-time maintenance of Yukon Corporate Online Registry.  

  • Corporate registry services are also accessible at the Government of Yukon’s Corporate Affairs branch office at 307 Black Street, 1st floor, Whitehorse.

  • Free training sessions will be available at 307 Black Street, Main Floor Boardroom, Whitehorse, Yukon: October 10 and 11 for law firms and Oct. 17 and 24 for Yukon and extra-territorial corporations.
